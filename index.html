The Story
Koshish is a line of handmade products crafted by 20 year-old Sahil. He uses materials such as jute and colourful wood to make various home accessories. Sahil suffers from Down’s Syndrome, which has resulted in physical and mental challenges – he learned how to craft paper bags and envelopes at a vocational centre which eventually led to an interest in handicrafts. His current offerings include table organizers, pen stands, bottle racks, utility baskets, coasters, mirrors, napkin holders and book racks.

He works 4-6 hours a day and finds his work exciting and fulfilling while also taking it very seriously. He crafts and sells these products independently with the help of his mother. He also loves painting, listening to music and dancing.

Sahil and his mother sell most of his products through local exhibitions that showcase eco-friendly and handmade goods. Unfortunately, this has proven to be an unsustainable business model for them as stalls at such events are often exorbitantly priced. Contacting them by phone or by email is the best way to directly support them and to purchase Sahil’s products.
